## Role and scope

- **Title:** GM – AI Products (also referred to as EGM – AI Products). Melbourne.
- **Scope:** Leads Xero’s AI-powered products. Over the last ~5 years his org has delivered cashflow predictions, auto bank reconciliation (ABR), and data extraction APIs.
- **JAX:** He and **Eitan Sharon** (SVP Data & Science) are the “under the hood” owners of JAX (Just Ask Xero) — turning requests into executable workflows, agentic capabilities, evolution toward an “AI financial superagent.”
- **Partners:** Works closely with **Lisa Huang** (SVP Product Management – Direct, Insights & AI) on product vision and Xerocon; with **Brett Edmonds** and others on agent/platform; with **Eitan** on data and science.

## Relevance to bills / agents

- **FY27 roadmap:** “Bill pay automation” is on the list of features the AI org tracks for usage/cost (with payment chasing, payroll automation, doc/info chasing, Abacus, etc.).
- **Agent vs workflow:** In workshop/design discussions he’s flagged the need to decide “which parts can be modelled as a **workflow** and what needs to be an **agent**” — and that “the whole agent could be set up as a temporal workflow” (architecture matters).
- **Worry:** “Work vs announcement” — he cares that we don’t over-promise; feasibility and technical constraints need to be in the room (aligns with Pratik’s “get AI in the workshops” ask).
- **Masterclass:** His “Building AI Products” talk stresses applied research, translation from research to product, evaluation and beta — so he thinks in terms of safe, measurable rollout.
