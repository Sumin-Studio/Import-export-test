# Sharing updates: Slack + Confluence (10 Mar 2026)

**Overview:** Short reference for how to share material updates so the right people see them. Use Slack for discovery and Confluence (or Miro) for the full detail.

## When you publish something material

1. **Post to the canonical channel** (e.g. Bill AI / payments-agents-team).
2. **Keep the post short** — readable in ~1 minute.
3. **Include one clear link** to the full content (Confluence page, Miro board, or repo doc).
4. **Tag people** who need to decide or be aware (`@name`).

## Template (copy and adapt)

```
[1–2 sentences: what changed or what you’re sharing.]

Key decision / ask: [one line if relevant]

Full detail: [Confluence / Miro / repo link]

@person1 @person2 [for decisions or awareness]
```

## Examples

- *Status update:* "Safety Shield working brief is updated with a UX risk register and overview block. Full brief: [link]. @Rory @Tauqir for awareness."
- *Decision needed:* "Proposing we add an Agents design sync (fortnightly, same crowd as Regional Crit). Details and cadence: [link]. @Angus @Audrey — thoughts?"
- *Video:* If you share a video update, add a **transcript or bullet summary** in the same thread or on the linked Confluence page so people who can’t watch still get the content.

## Why this works

- **Short post** = more people read it; **link** = depth for those who need it.
- **@mentions** force the right eyes (per Michael’s approach).
- **Transcript for video** = accessibility and quick scan (per Angus / Jess).
