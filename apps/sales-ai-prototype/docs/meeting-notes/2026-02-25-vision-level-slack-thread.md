# Vision Level Discussion — Slack Thread (24-25 Feb 2026)

**Participants:** Jon Bell, Brett Edmonds, David Brown, Chong Xu, Angus Tait, Jenny Nedanovski

---

## Jon's Proposed Structure (from Brett alignment meeting)

Three hero images on a single slide — intent, not mechanics:
1. An alert on a phone: "bills have been paid. Click to learn more."
2. An alert on WhatsApp: "items have been auto-approved. Click to learn more."
3. A conversation in ChatGPT where someone magically pays from chat without a bill.

Then come prepared with details. Candy first, vegetables second.

Referenced Bing pitch at Microsoft: one slide, three images (concert = Bing Audio, menu in French = Bing Vision, web search = Bing Search). Also referenced Peter O'Malley UK Lending pitch that Diya loved — same structure.

## Brett's Position

- Don't lead with a version that assumes users have little-to-no trust
- "If that's what you lead with, the story becomes 'AI you can't trust and still have to check'"
- Lead with what it looks like once users trust it and sensible policies are in place
- Work with Soonee's team on feasibility — "there's a risk that what you think is hard is actually an easy science problem"
- Test: "Can Diya get up on Xerocon main stage and announce this idea and get a standing ovation?"
- Shared Ubiquitous Language Guide: "A virtual entity that perceives its environment, reasons within defined Policies, and executes Agent Actions toward a defined Goal"

## Chong's Position

- Agrees with hero approach but wants users in the driver seat
- "3 critical bills you need to pay this week" with agent reasoning behind it
- Two novel AI applications: (1) LLM for personalized human explanations, (2) Agentic application where agent does heavy lifting between clicks
- Proposed AB flow (review → update plan → summarize email for SB approval) and SB flow (open email → review → one-click handle)

## Brett's Contradiction Flag

- Chong message 1: "fully autonomous vision is great but I don't think we can get there at day 1"
- Chong message 2: "agent goes ahead to prepare the bill prep screens... getting approvals and making payments"
- Brett: "I'm trying to understand the difference"
- Jon bridged: Chong tends conservative, wording gets muddied. Approach: "the best thing is x, and I think I'm hearing you say you agree as long as y?"

## Jenny's Input

> "I like the framing, it communicates intent, not mechanics, and that's what gets alignment. Where I think we'll need to have a think about is in how we bridge vision to credibility."

## David's Input

> "Think of this as a Circus tent. The above is the banner (new headline show in town), a moment to hook, intrigue. But we need to make sure we have main event needs i.e our use cases and mechanics."

## Jon's Push Back

> "'Here are three critical bills' is not an agent. It's not interesting. It does not hit the bar of something Diya would be proud to show off. It is the definition of what she is asking us not to do."

Structure that works (proven with UK Lending):
1. Single, long term, moonshot hero image — the vision
2. Second slide — foundational technologies needed
3. Third slide — deeper into foundations to prove homework done

## Resolution

Team in wild agreement on:
- Three heroes work as the hook
- Then explain mechanics
- Jon pushing to keep ambition high, not lead with conservative "here are 3 bills" framing
- 3 hours of drop-in meetings booked for Thursday
