## Safety Shield framing

- Core customer outcome: **protect customer cash flow from adverse impacts**.
- Three current risk categories:
  - **Fraud detection**
  - **Duplicate bill identification**
  - **Cash flow risk** such as budget overruns or stretching the business too far
- Current preferred journey:
  1. **Monitor**
  2. **Detect / flag**
  3. **Provide evidence**
  4. **Take action**
- Strong preference for a **human-in-the-loop** model rather than full autonomy, especially in early iterations.

## Competitive and product insights

- Duplicate detection and audit logging look like table stakes across the market.
- Stronger differentiation opportunities appear to be:
  - anomalous / fraudulent invoice detection
  - bank-account-detail change monitoring
  - network-powered fraud intelligence across AP, AR, and bank data
  - making action-taking easy rather than only surfacing alerts
- QuickBooks and Sage appear weaker at bill-specific protection and more focused on GL-level controls.
- Open framing question remains: what truly needs to be branded or treated as an "agent" versus simply being a high-value feature. The pragmatic view in the session was that customer value matters more than strict purity of the label.
