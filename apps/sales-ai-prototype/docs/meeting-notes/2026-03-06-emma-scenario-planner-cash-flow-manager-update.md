# Emma update — Scenario Planner vs Cash Flow Manager (6 Mar 2026)

**Source:** Robb Schiller (Slack), relaying Emma Dalman. Date: 6 Mar 2026.

**Why captured:** Clarifies related product boundaries for Bill Cashflow / JAX. The agents huddle (6 Mar) called out Scenario Planner and Cash Flow Manager as related product; this doc spells out purpose, timeframes, and overlap so the team can align handoff and messaging.

---

## Emma’s update (summary)

- **JAX and Syft:** Future cash flow responses in JAX are being provided using the Syft-created **Cash Flow Manager** feature, separate from scenario planning.

### Cash Flow Manager

- **Purpose:** Help customers understand if they can pay their bills.
- **Timeframe:** Up to the next 180 days.
- **Basis:** Upcoming invoices and bills, plus other future transactions — all based on **individual transactions on a day-by-day basis**.

### Scenario Planner

- **Purpose:** Model potential future states of the business profit and loss over the next 1–2 years. Help customers see the business impact of different decisions (grow the business or avoid risks).
- **Timeframe:** 1–2 years.
- **Basis:** **Chart of accounts, month-by-month** — a more aggregated level than Cash Flow Manager.

### Overlap and open question

- For scenario planning to show holistic impact of decisions, **impact to future cash flow** matters. The team is investigating whether Cash Flow Manager can be the source data for this side of scenario planning. It is unclear whether account-level scenarios can be applied to a transaction-based cash flow forecast.

### Intent for customers

- There is some overlap, but the core purpose and approach of each tool are quite different. The aim is to make these differences and similarities clear to the customer and to provide **seamless handoff** between each feature depending on what the user is trying to achieve.
