3. **System of Engagement** -- Fast onboarding, eliminate DIY config, AI-native bill experience

## Pete's Apron Research (UK)

Key finding from AB interviews about why they use Apron (integrated to Xero) instead of core Xero:
- ABs were the ones who suggested Apron to clients and set it up
- Core workflow solved: back-and-forth between SB and AB to capture and code bills
- One AB prepared bill runs considering cashflow, got SB to approve
- Both ABs offered Apron for free as value-add, absorbing costs
- **Competitive angle**: build this into Xero = one less third-party service for ABs

Three user types to design for:
1. SB doing it themselves (save time to grow business)
2. AB doing it on behalf of SB (save time to scale practice)
3. "Do it together" workflows (reduce back-and-forth friction)

## Pratik's "Map vs Territory" Insight

> The biggest myth at Xero is that the accounting platform is an accurate representation of a business's operations. It's often not.

Examples:
- Construction: trade accounts and supplier credit codified in contracts but never in Xero -- cash flow view is inaccurate
- Inventory: POs, deliveries, quality, disputes tracked in ERPs like Cin7 but warnings never reach Xero

> We shouldn't be constrained by the data we have in our platform; it can often be misleading or incomplete.
