# 2026-03-11

## What was discussed

- Dillon refined the **Bill Cashflow agent** around two explicit goals (maintain a buffer of X days over Y, or else keep the business above zero as long as possible) and four core scenarios: temporary buffer dip (V-shape), ongoing shortfall (L-shape heading toward overdraft), healthy business with surplus buffer, and an unsolvable “business is failing” case where no bill reshuffling avoids going negative.
- Rory and the Safety Shield team shared a structured **Safety Shield Target Scenarios** Confluence page, defining risk categories (fraud, duplicates, anomalous/erroneous), concrete detection scenarios (first-time vendor with large bill, abnormal supplier spikes, exact/fuzzy duplicates, bank-detail changes, PO/receipt mismatches, velocity anomalies, fabricated invoices), and captured feedback on PO usage, risk/compliance involvement, and ML vs rules.
- Tauqir, Mugdha, and Lili aligned that Safety Shield and related agent work will produce **ADRs** reviewed by Leads and a Principal Engineer, following patterns from Making Payments and CashGPT, with final sign-off expectations still being clarified for these cross-squad agents.
- Areti asked for a single PRD for all agents; Jenny clarified that the original combined PRD has been split into separate PRDs (Just Pay, Safety Shield, Bill Runner, Onboarding), and Jon created a **Payments Agents Hub** in Confluence to organise docs by agent rather than squad.
- Jon captured and shared a **high-level Payments agents suite summary** (Bill · Cash Flow, Bill · Workflow 1 Safety Shield, Bill · Workflow 2 Just Pay, Approvals Foundations, Smart Payment Request Agent) as reusable narrative copy for PMs, which is now stored as a reference doc in this repo.
- Jon and Chong replaced the large, cross-timezone weekly agents meeting with a pattern of **daily async squad updates** plus two smaller NH/SH **Show & Tell** meetings focused on artifacts and recorded demos, to make synchronous time more valuable and reduce “this could have been a Slack message” risk.

- **Rory PRD sparring (Chong, Jenny):** Rory walked through the Safety Shield PRD; Chong and Jenny are aligned on short-term surfacing (Bills List + Bill Detail), SMB + AB target segment, AB cross-client risky bills as future state, and considering exposure of Safety Shield data to the **Abacus** agent platform for ABs. ## Decisions

- Bill Cashflow modeling and UX should, at minimum, be able to explain and act within the four scenarios Dillon outlined, with “maintain buffer” and “stay above zero” as clear goals rather than implicit heuristics.
- Safety Shield will continue to evolve its scenario catalog in Confluence, but the initial repo-facing framing focuses on high-confidence, explainable detection cases (duplicates, first-time large bills, abnormal supplier spikes, and key fraud signals) before more complex ML-driven scenarios.
- ADRs for this agent workstream will be treated as **first-class architectural artefacts**, consulted with Leads and PEs rather than being informal notes.
- Agents documentation should be **organised by agent**, not by squad, with the Payments Agents Hub in Confluence acting as the entry point for PRDs and key artefacts.
- The weekly all-hands agents meeting is retired in favour of **async status** plus targeted Show & Tell sessions, with an expectation that squads still post daily updates in-channel (including “nothing new to report” when applicable).
