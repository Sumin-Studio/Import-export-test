# Bills JBTD arc — and where your three concepts sit

**Purpose:** Use Xero’s Jobs to be Done (JBTD) / Customer Journey Framework (CJF) so you can position your three concepts (Bill Payment Planner, Intelligent Bill Approval, Just Pay) in the customer arc. You’re not owning the full map; this is the framing you need to “touch on” JBTD and show where your proposal fits.

## The JBTD arc (Pay Out / bills) in Xero language

Xero’s CJF uses **Level 0** (lifecycle stage), **Level 1** (large job), **Level 2** (job steps). For bills, the relevant L2 jobs are:

| CJF code | Job | Definition (from CJF / Glean) |
|----------|-----|-------------------------------|
| **L2.7B.1** | Receive invoices/bills | Receiving and tracking invoices from suppliers or partners. Receiving invoices for products bought and/or services received. Visibility of when bills are due and when money is owed. |
| **L2.7B.2** | Pay bills | Making bill payments (to suppliers or partners). |

These sit under **L1.7C Record keeping** (reconciling bank, capturing source docs, managing AP/AR and payments so financials are accurate) and the broader **L0.7 Manage accounts, cash flow and finance** context.

The **Bills User Journey** work (Angus, Robb, Purchases Design Sync) breaks the flow into more granular **job steps** (verb + object, product-agnostic). A typical Pay Out arc looks like this:

1. **Receive bill** — Get the bill in (email, upload, connect).
2. **Prepare bills to pay** / **Understand what to pay** — Triage, which bills, what’s due, what’s urgent.
3. **Decide when to pay** — When to pay (this week, next week), cash position, prioritisation.
4. **Get approval** (when needed) — Second pair of eyes, risk check, authorisation.
5. **Schedule / select for payment** — Choose bills, set date, funding.
6. **Make payment** — Execute payment.
7. **Record / track payment** — Reconciliation, status, audit.

Peter’s point: the **tasks** don’t radically change (SB wants to get paid, customer needs to pay). The shift we’re proposing is **how** they’re done: *from laborious and emotional to as simple as a prompt or a click.*

## Where your three concepts sit in the arc

| Your concept | JBTD / job step | What job we’re helping with |
|--------------|------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Bill Payment Planner** | **Prepare bills to pay** + **Decide when to pay** (and into **Schedule**) | “Which bills to pay this week and when.” We recommend; you approve. Cuts cognitive load and clicks. Fits **L2.7B.2 Pay bills** — the planning/prioritisation slice. |
| **Intelligent Bill Approval** | **Get approval** | “When bills need a second pair of eyes.” We flag risk and route to the right person; they approve in WhatsApp. Fits **L2.7B.2 Pay bills** — the approval slice. |
| **Just Pay** | Shortcut into **Make payment** (often skipping or compressing **Receive** → **Prepare** → **Decide** → **Approve**) | “Pay someone without creating a bill first.” Say it → we create the record and pay. Still **L2.7B.2 Pay bills** but a different entry: pay-first, minimal prior steps. |

**Bill enrichment** (channel’s “zero bill entry” / one-click entry) sits in **Receive bill** / **L2.7B.1 Receive invoices/bills** — getting the bill into the system with one click. Your three concepts don’t own that step; you can name it as “earlier in the arc” and point to the same Bills theme.

## One-slide / one-paragraph positioning (for your vision)

**Option A — One paragraph**

*“Our bills proposal sits in the Pay Out job: **L2.7B.2 Pay bills**. We’re not changing the job — the customer still needs to pay bills. We’re changing how it’s done. **Bill Payment Planner** helps them **prepare and decide when** to pay (we recommend, they approve in 3 clicks). **Intelligent Bill Approval** handles the **get approval** step when bills need a second pair of eyes (we flag risk and send to the approver in WhatsApp). **Just Pay** lets them **pay** without the full receive–prepare–approve flow (say it, we create and pay). Earlier in the same arc, **bill enrichment** (one-click entry) lives in **L2.7B.1 Receive invoices/bills**. How this connects to the rest of the bills journey is being aligned in the Bills × Making payments working group.”*

**Option B — One slide (bullet form)**

- **CJF:** L2.7B.1 Receive invoices/bills → L2.7B.2 Pay bills.
- **Arc:** Receive → Prepare/understand → Decide when → Approve (if needed) → Schedule → Make payment → Record/track.
- **Our three:**  
  - **Planner** = Prepare + Decide when (we recommend, you approve).  
  - **Approval** = Get approval (we flag, we route, approver taps in WhatsApp).  
  - **Just Pay** = Make payment with minimal prior steps (say it → we create + pay).
- **Shift:** Same jobs; from laborious and emotional to as simple as a prompt or a click.
